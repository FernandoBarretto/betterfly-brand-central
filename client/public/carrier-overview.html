Why Now: The industry inflection point

Employee benefit costs continue rising and the industry's operating model remains reactive: pay after the event, then renegotiate at renewal. Carriers have deep claims data, but it's inherently lagging—by the time a claim appears, the risk has materialized. Wellness platforms generate engagement but can't tie it to insurance outcomes. This fragmentation keeps the industry in a renewal-centric loop where differentiation collapses into price.

Operational Model:
No Overlap, No Channel Conflict

The carrier owns underwriting, policy issuance, and claims. The broker retains the employer relationship and remains broker-of-record. Betterfly operates the experience layer—broker portal, employer portal, member app—and the data engine. No overlap, no channel conflict. Revenue shared through a flexible PEPM model. Betterfly is ISO 27001 certified with enterprise-grade data security practices. HIPAA-compliant architecture for U.S. operations. SOC 2 Type II planned. Clear separation of regulated and non-regulated data flows between carrier and platform.

Product Scope

A clean, broker-familiar portfolio built for speed and adoption. Core offering includes Group Life (basic employer-paid with AD&D + voluntary buy-up), Voluntary Benefits (Accident, Critical Illness, Cancer, Hospital Indemnity), and Individual Life (simplified term for SMB owners and key employees). This maximizes adoption, minimizes operational burden, and creates the data foundation for future expansion. Near term expansion considers Dental, Vision, and Income Protection (STD/LTD), to further strengthen the platform's value proposition while maintaining operational simplicity.
